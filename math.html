Talks


  • Course Presentation on Forcing Axioms (University of Toronto, 2024): A proof that under MA, every locally finite, outer regular, Radon measure is σ-finite; presented in a graduate course on forcing.
  • An Introduction to Descriptive Set Theory (University of Toronto Scarborough CMS Undergraduate Seminar, 2023): A crash course on Polish spaces, followed by an introduction to the notions of measure and category, with a discussion of how classical theorems on Polish spaces can be used to prove the Erdős-Sierpiński duality between measure and category. The recording is available here.
  • Basic Embedding Results in Descriptive Set Theory (University of Toronto Mississauga, 2023): A brief introduction to Polish spaces, followed by a discussion of classical embedding results involving the the Hilbert Cube, the Cantor space, and the Baire space.

Notes & Writing


  • The Continuum Hypothesis and the Calkin Algebra (December 2023): A brief overview of the Calkin algebra on a Hilbert space, and Phillips and Weaver's argument that the continuum hypothesis allows a construction of an outer automorphism on it. Written as a project for a graduate course on K-theory and C*-algebras taught by Professor George Elliott.
  • Connecting C*-Algebras with Model Theory (October 2023): A short introduction to continuous model theory and its application in axiomatization of classes of C*-algebras. Written as a project for a graduate course on K-theory and C*-algebras taught by Professor George Elliott.
  • The Continuum Hypothesis (April 2023): A collection of solutions to and discussion of exercises from Chapter 17 of Just & Weese's book Discovering Modern Set Theory. II: Set-Theoretic Tools for Every Mathematician. Written as a project for a reading course on set theory taught by Professor Stevo Todorčević.

My Bookshelf


Here is a list of books from which I learned math, sorted by topic. By no means is it comprehensive, but the books listed here are my go-to recommendations for their respective topics:


  • Calculus: Calculus by Michael Spivak
  • Linear Algebra: Linear Algebra Done Right by Sheldon Axler
  • Multivariable Calculus: Calculus on Manifolds by Michael Spivak, and Analysis on Manifolds by James Munkres
  • Elementary Number Theory: Elementary Number Theory by Gareth A. Jones and J. Mary Jones
  • Elementary Combinatorics: Applied Combinatorics by Mitchel T. Keller and William T. Trotter
  • Logic: A Friendly Introduction to Mathematical Logic by Christopher C. Leary and Lars Kristiansen
  • Model Theory: Model Theory by Chen Chung Chang and Howard Jerome Keisler
  • Topology: Topology by James Munkres
  • Real Analysis: Real Mathematical Analysis by Charles C. Pugh
  • Complex Analysis: Elementary Theory of Analytic Functions of One Or Several Complex Variables by Henri Cartan
  • Abstract Algebra: Abstract Algebra by David Steven Dummit and Richard M. Foote, and Abstract Algebra by Pierre Antoine Grillet
  • Classical Differential Geometry: Differential Geometry of Curves and Surfaces by Kristopher Tapp
  • Measure Theory: Real Analysis: Modern Techniques and Their Applications by Gerald B. Folland, and Real Analysis: Measure Theory, Integration, and Hilbert Spaces by Elias Stein and Rami Shakarchi
  • Functional Analysis: A Course in Functional Analysis by John B. Conway
  • Set Theory: Set Theory by Kenneth Kunen, and Discovering Modern Set Theory by Winfried Just and Martin Weese

For those interested, here are some recommended advanced references on set theory and C*-algebras:


  • Set Theory by Thomas Jech
  • Classical Descriptive Set Theory by Alexander S. Kechris
  • Handbook of Set-Theoretic Topology by Kenneth Kunen
  • Notes on Forcing Axioms by Stevo Todorčević
  • An Introduction to K-Theory for C*-algebras by Mikael Rørdam
  • Combinatorial Set Theory of C*-algebras by Ilijas Farah
